# UI Direction

## Feel
- Fun, playful, and motivating
- Friendly and modern, not childish
- Focused enough for learning, not visually noisy

## Visual Priorities
- Strong color hierarchy with bright accents
- Large readable typography for Japanese and English text
- Clear progress states, streaks, XP, badges, and completion feedback
- Card-based layouts with clean spacing and strong tap targets
- Mobile-first interactions and fast scanning

## Experience Priorities
- Lessons should feel short and satisfying
- Reward correct answers and completion moments
- Make progress visible at all times
- Reduce friction between opening the app and starting a study task

## Motion
- Use small celebratory animation for wins, unlocks, streaks, and level-ups
- Keep transitions smooth and lightweight
- Motion should support delight, not slow down practice

## Avoid
- Generic SaaS dashboard styling
- Dense walls of text
- Flat, lifeless layouts
- Overuse of dark, serious, or academic visual tone
- Animations that compete with learning content
